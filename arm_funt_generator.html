Arm Assembly Function Generator

Design for generating AC signal based on inputs

Project Overview

In this project, I programmed an STM32 using ARM Assembly to output an AC signal based on voltage inputs from two potentiometers. The design allows me to take in the voltage from the pot, and output the corresponding amplitude and frequency of the signal.

Implementation Details

In this project, I developed an embedded system to generate and manipulate waveforms using an STM32 microcontroller, focusing on digital-to-analog conversion (DAC) and direct digital synthesis (DDS). I implemented assembly routines to calculate phase increments based on a desired frequency and sample rate, enabling the system to iterate through a 1024-entry wave table. I decided to use phase tracking in "16ths" to maintain precision, successfully producing waveforms such as sine and sawtooth waves, which were verified on an oscilloscope at frequencies around 585Hz to 597Hz. In addition to controlling the frequency of the signal, I also used analog-to-digital conversion (ADC) to allow for real-time hardware interaction. I configured ADC channels (specifically channels 5 and 6) to read values from potentiometers on a breadboard. These readings were used as scalars to dynamically adjust the gain and frequency through a continuous read loop in my main assembly code. This integration demonstrated a complete signal chain where physical analog inputs directly influenced the digital generation of audio signals.
